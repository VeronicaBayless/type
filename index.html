Introduction

The designer Joshua Darden

Joshua Darden is the first well-known African American typeface designer. He designed his first typeface at only fifteen years old. He is often referred to as a “silent hunter” or “mythic”. He earned his well-earned free time after a successful career. His typefaces are used by W magazine, AT&T, Crayola, and Huggies. He was named one of Print Magazine’s “New Visual Artist” in 2006. Darden founded his own studio called Darden Studio in 2004 and sold it to a long-time colleague Joyce Ketterer in 2019 but the name remained Darden Studio.

Freight Text is a family of 24 fonts designed by Joshua Darden from The Freight Collection. Freight Text is the main text design for the collection. Freight Text is a serif typeface that was first published by GarageFonts in 2005. It has a humanistic aesthetic that has found wide popularity. It was designed to be viewed for long periods of time in documents, novels, and web pages.
